Python Image Manipulation Project

This Python project was created by Omer Ahmer (me) and my partner Thomas Andrews and was for our Computer Science Principles class. This project spanned from March 4, 2019 to March 25, 2019. This project was my third experience programming in Python, after doing a Hangman and Text Adventure project. For this project, we had to choose from 4 clients each with different expectations in editing their photos. The client Thomas and I chose was the second client, the family. The client wanted "a standard frame applied to a large number of pictures that feature one or more of the family members. They want the composite image to be memorable and to incorporate some personalized symbol, image, or silhouette that represents the interests of the family member(s). My partner and I experienced many difficulties and setbacks because we had no immediate person to help us in our programming. This project taught my partner and I how to solve problems using logic and help from our classmates instead of our teacher. I am especially proud of this project not only because of our end result, but that we were able to create a great result through all of the hardships we endured. We also learned a lifelong skill, which is being able to not give up and persevere. We also gained immense knowledge in Python libraries, especially libraries relating to image manipulation. Finally, we learned to consult our classmates in the rare case that we don't know how to get our result we desire.

App Inventor Project

This App Inventor Project was created by me and my partner Riya and was for our Computer Science Principles class. This project spanned from September 18, 2018, to October 5, 2018. For this project, we had to take an App Inventor original tutorial and modify it to make it our own. This time, I had to take charge of the physics and visuals of the game, while Riya had to write the documentation. We chose to do Mole Mash, since there were many things we could change. For our beta version, we added a pause, play, and reset button. We also added a mole which you were not supposed to touch, which was a picture of Mr. Brown. We had a few bugs, such as the moles overlapping and the play button refusing to work. We got feedback from our friends, and they said we had to fix some minor bugs they noticed and add some more of our own functions to make it more unique. To do that, we also added a slower and faster button so the moles move at different speeds. Also, we got rid of the fixed holes so that the moles could move freely on the screen and would never overlap. From this project, I learned how to take lead of a project and help my partner understand how everything works. I also learned how to take feedback and turn it into useful work, and lead by example.
